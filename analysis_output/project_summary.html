1Ô∏è‚É£ Overall Market Analysis

What I noticed

District school enrollment has been dropping steadily: 39k fewer students from 2018 to 2022 (about 6% overall). Meanwhile, charter schools as a whole are growing. Other charters picked up 16k students in the same period. Success Academy peaked in 2020 at ~16k students but has been flat or slightly down since.

SA schools also run larger than average (492-589 students vs. ~420-450 for district schools). Probably intentional, but it means they feel any dips more acutely.

What else would help?

The data doesn't tell us why families are choosing (or leaving) SA. It would be useful to have application/waitlist numbers, student retention by grade, and some geographic context (like which neighborhoods have the most demand vs. capacity.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Enrollment Projections (2023-2027)

Approach

I used a simple 3-year average growth rate based on 2020-2022 data. This gives about -5.2% per year, which continues the recent downward trend. I avoided the 2018-2019 numbers because those are pre-pandemic and mixing them in would muddy the signal.

The caveat

This is a rough projection. It assumes current trends continue as-is. In reality, SA could open new schools, improve retention, or face more competition. Without application/waitlist data, we can't tell if the decline is a supply problem (fewer schools) or demand problem (fewer applicants).

Gaps in the data

Application volume trends, age-cohort data from the city, and student attrition rates by grade. SA's own expansion plans would also help contextualize this.

üìä projections.csv

Calculated 3-year rolling growth rate and applied it forward:

def project_enrollment(df, years_to_project=5): sa_data = df[df['School_Type'] == 'SA'] yearly = sa_data.groupby('Year')['Total Enrollment'].sum() # Use last 3 years to get growth rate recent_years = yearly.tail(3) growth_rates = recent_years.pct_change().dropna() avg_growth_rate = growth_rates.mean() # -5.2% # Build out projections current = yearly.iloc[-1] for i in range(1, years_to_project + 1): current = current * (1 + avg_growth_rate) projections['Year'].append(last_year + i) projections['Projected_Enrollment'].append(round(current)) # save_outputs() projections['projections'].to_csv(output_dir / 'projections.csv', index=False)

3Ô∏è‚É£ Dashboard Requirements

Who's using this?

The main users would be school leaders and the enrollment team who need to track their numbers daily/weekly. Regional superintendents would check in periodically to compare schools. Leadership (CEO, COO) would want high-level rollups, and finance/marketing would use it for budget and campaign planning.

What to show

Type Metrics
Core numbers Total enrollment, fill rate, applications received, yield rate, retention
Trends YoY change, weekly application volume, waitlist movement
Comparisons Actual vs target by school, by grade, by demographic
Red flags Under-capacity grades, application drop-offs, unusual withdrawal spikes

Design notes

Keep it simple: cards at top for KPIs, line charts for trends, bar charts for school comparisons. Should work on mobile since school leaders are often on the go. Color-code by school type (SA vs. charter vs. district) for quick scanning.
